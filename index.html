A Midsummer Night's Dream: Athens Daily Chronicle

Act Summaries

Act I: "A Tangled Web in Athens"

The Duke of Athens is to marry the Queen of the Amazons. Egeus demands that his daughter Hermia obeys Athenian law and marry Demetrius, though she loves Lysander. Hermia and Lysander plan to elope while Helena, in love with Demetrius, decides to reveal their plan to him.

Act II: "Enchanted Errors Begin"

In the forest, the fairy king and queen, Oberon and Titania, quarrel. Oberon enlists Puck to use a love potion on Titania and the Athenian lovers. Puck mistakes the Athenians, causing Lysander to love Helena.

Act III: "The Play Within the Play"

The mechanicals rehearse their play. Puck bewitches Bottom with a donkey’s head, causing Titania to fall in love with him. The lovers’ quarrel escalates under the potion's influence.

Act IV: "Lovers and Fools"

Oberon reverses the potion's effects. Theseus finds the couples and sanctions the marriages. Bottom returns to Athens with no memory of the night's events.

Act V: "Wedding and a Play"

The three couples are married. The mechanicals perform their play, which is met with amusement and ridicule from the nobles.
